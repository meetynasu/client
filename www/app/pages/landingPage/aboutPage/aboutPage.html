History

Ynaiasu : “meet with someone” in the indigenous Chibcha language. That is the idea behind Ynasu. We are creating a direct marketplace where agricultural producers and buyers can meet virtually, connect, and even trade directly. Ynasu started as an idea late 2015 in Medellin, Colombia. At the time, a 50- year civil conflict was ending in the country, a conflict which had forced many farmers to grow coca plants. But with this conflict’s end in sight, cocoa was seen as the ideal substitute and symbolizes the post-conflict economy in Colombia. After having the opportunity to meet with cacao farmers on site and hearing about the challenges of both chocolate makers and cocoa producers, we noticed the missing link between farmers and buyers. We decided to build an online marketplace where the two could meet, connect and trade directly, facilitating transparency and traceability of the food supply chain. Ynasu was born.

Mission

We want the distance to become irrelevant in the trading of agricultural commodities

Although the world is increasingly globalized and interconnected thanks to technology, cocoa transactions are still largely conducted in an archaic trading manner: the need for intermediaries, a lack of transparency, and traceability. Many producers do not have access to the necessary and sufficient information to determine the price they should sell for. Furthermore prices are influenced by speculation and arbitrary trading, which has profound impacts on farmers, [but with no strong logical reasoning]. It is time to change this way of thinking. We believe a system of direct and fair trade is the future for agricultural trade and a way to improve farmer livelihood.

FAIR

Buyers decide what price the cacao is worth taking into account the quality and also the social-economic aims of the sellers which we publish. This price is agreed independently with the seller.

DIRECT

We only work with verified associations, cooperatives and select independent producers; you can be assured that the price you agree to pay the farmers will actually go to the farmers!

TRANSPARENT

Buyers and sellers will receive a breakdown of every associated cost. This helps both sides understand supply chain costs. Nothing is hidden!

Vision

To extend to other agricultural commodities and countries

Farmers produce our food, and through their processes can take care of the land or continue to farm in ways that will be to our entire future detriment. But the more we squeeze their prices, the more pressure they feel, and the less they are able to and worry about taking care of the earth and maintain its viability; resulting in the use of more artificial fertilizers and pesticides. In turn these artificial fertilizers and pesticides are bad for our health. We care about our health, but it’s also time we all cared a bit more not just about our own health, but the health of the entire food ecosystem and those who are fundamental to it. At Ynasu, we want to make it easier for those [food makers] who want to source fairly and directly to do so and also encourage more to do the same.] We have a vision to combine a system of fair and direct trade where farmers can receive meaningful premiums for their products, be empowered to take charge of their own businesses and see the value in sustainable development. But this can only be achieved together. Help us help them. Colombia is the perfect place to start; it is the country with the second highest biodiversity in the world and has an abundance of other agricultural commodities like panela, coffee, rubber etc... With this biodiversity, it has the most to gain from a fair and direct trade system which encourages sustainable development practices.
